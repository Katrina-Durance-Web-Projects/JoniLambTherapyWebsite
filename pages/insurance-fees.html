Tips for Using Your Health Insurance for Mental Health Care
Call the member services phone number on the back of your insurance card and ask the following questions to understand your “behavioral health benefits” (or mental health coverage).
  • Ask to be transferred to the ‘behavioral health department’ as many insurance plans separate physical health and mental health benefits.

  • What is my deductible for behavioral health? A deductible is the amount of money your plan requires you to pay out of pocket for services before your benefits ‘kick in’.

  • What is my co-pay or co-insurance? A co-pay is a flat fee per visit and co-insurance is a percentage of the cost of the total visit. You will pay only your co-pay or co-insurance after you have paid down your deductible.

  • Do I have a session limit?

  • Do I need a referral from my primary care doctor to see a therapist or psychiatrist?

  • May I have a list of local ‘in network’ providers emailed to me? If in network options are not available nearby, most plans offer out of network benefits (typically associated with a higher deductible and higher co-pays or co-insurance).

* During the Covid-19 crisis, access to in person therapeutic or psychiatric services may be unavailable. Please ask your insurance carrier if "teletherapy":

  • is covered and if coverage is temporary

  • is covered at the same benefits as in-person behavioral health
